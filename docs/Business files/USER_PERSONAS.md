# User Personas
## Clariva Care - AI Receptionist Bot for Doctors

---

## Primary Persona: Dr. Priya (Solo Practitioner)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 32-45 years
- **Location:** Tier 1-2 cities in India (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Pune, Hyderabad)
- **Practice Type:** Solo clinic or small practice (1-3 doctors)
- **Medical Experience:** 5-15 years post-graduation
- **Specialization:** General practice, dermatology, gynecology, pediatrics, or other OPD specialties
- **Monthly Income:** ₹50,000 - ₹2,00,000
- **Education:** MBBS, possibly MD/MS
- **Practice Setup:** Own clinic or shared clinic space

### Psychographics
- **Tech Comfort:** Tech-savvy but not a developer - comfortable with smartphones, apps, social media
- **Mindset:** Growth-oriented, wants to build practice, values efficiency
- **Work Style:** Hands-on, involved in all aspects of practice
- **Social Media Attitude:** Active user, sees value in social media for practice growth
- **Innovation:** Willing to try new tools if they solve real problems
- **Time Management:** Struggles with time, wants to focus on patient care

### Behavior Patterns

**Social Media Usage:**
- Posts health tips, educational content 2-3 times per week
- Engages with followers (likes, comments, responds to DMs)
- Uses Instagram primarily, Facebook secondary
- Gets 20-50 inquiries per week on social media
- Spends 5-10 hours per week managing social media presence
- Responds to messages but often delayed or misses some

**Practice Management:**
- Manages own appointments (or has 1 receptionist)
- Handles patient inquiries personally
- Uses WhatsApp for patient communication
- Keeps basic patient records (paper or simple software)
- Works 6-8 hours/day seeing patients
- Spends additional 2-3 hours on admin tasks

**Technology Usage:**
- Uses smartphone daily for communication
- Comfortable with social media platforms
- May use basic practice management software
- Uses payment apps (PhonePe, GPay)
- Not very technical but can learn new tools

### Pain Points

**1. Inquiry Overload (HIGH PRIORITY)**
- Receives 20-50+ patient inquiries per week on social media
- Can't respond to all messages/comments
- Feels overwhelmed by volume
- Worries about missing potential patients
- **Impact:** Losing 30-50% of potential patients

**2. Time Consumption (HIGH PRIORITY)**
- Spends 5-10 hours/week responding to social media inquiries
- Manual appointment booking is time-consuming
- Takes time away from patient care
- Works evenings/weekends on social media
- **Impact:** Burnout, less time for family, reduced patient care quality

**3. Inconsistent Response (MEDIUM PRIORITY)**
- Sometimes responds quickly, sometimes delayed
- Can't respond 24/7 (only during working hours)
- Patients get frustrated with slow responses
- Loses patients to competitors who respond faster
- **Impact:** Poor patient experience, reputation damage

**4. No Tracking (MEDIUM PRIORITY)**
- Doesn't know which inquiries convert to appointments
- Can't measure ROI of social media efforts
- Doesn't track inquiry sources
- Can't optimize marketing strategy
- **Impact:** Wasted marketing efforts, unclear what works

**5. Professional Boundaries (LOW PRIORITY)**
- Worries about saying wrong thing on social media
- Privacy concerns (patient information)
- Maintaining professional tone
- Compliance concerns
- **Impact:** Stress, risk of violations

### Goals & Motivations

**Primary Goals:**
1. **Never miss a patient inquiry** - Every inquiry is a potential patient
2. **Save time** - Automate routine tasks, focus on patient care
3. **Grow practice** - Convert more social media followers to patients
4. **Maintain professional image** - Professional, consistent communication
5. **Work-life balance** - Reduce evening/weekend work on social media

**Motivations:**
- Wants to build successful practice
- Values patient relationships
- Wants to be efficient and productive
- Seeks tools that make life easier
- Open to technology that solves real problems

### Technology Comfort Level
- **Smartphone Apps:** Very comfortable (uses daily)
- **Social Media:** Very comfortable (active user)
- **Web Applications:** Comfortable (can learn)
- **Complex Software:** Less comfortable (needs simple UI)
- **Coding/Technical:** Not comfortable (needs no-code solutions)

### Decision-Making Factors

**What Influences Purchase:**
1. **Solves real problem** - Must address inquiry overload
2. **Easy to use** - Simple setup, intuitive interface
3. **Affordable** - Within budget (₹500-2000/month acceptable)
4. **Trustworthy** - From credible company, good reviews
5. **Quick ROI** - See value within first week

**Purchase Process:**
1. Sees problem (inquiry overload)
2. Researches solutions (Google, social media, recommendations)
3. Tries free trial or demo
4. Evaluates based on ease of use and results
5. Decides based on value vs cost

### Quote (Representative)
*"I get so many messages on Instagram every day - patients asking about appointments, symptoms, fees. I try to respond to all of them, but I just can't. I know I'm losing patients because I can't respond fast enough or sometimes miss messages entirely. I need something that handles this automatically so I can focus on seeing patients."*

### Use Case Scenario

**Typical Day:**
- Morning: Checks Instagram, sees 10+ new messages from overnight
- Spends 30 minutes responding to urgent ones
- Sees patients 9 AM - 2 PM
- Afternoon: Checks social media again, more messages
- Spends another 30 minutes responding
- Evening: Posts health tip, gets more inquiries
- Feels overwhelmed, some messages go unanswered

**With Clariva Care:**
- Morning: Bot has handled all overnight inquiries
- Sees 3 new appointments booked automatically
- Reviews patient info in dashboard (2 minutes)
- Sees patients 9 AM - 2 PM
- Afternoon: Checks dashboard, sees 2 more bookings
- Evening: Posts health tip, bot handles all new inquiries
- Feels in control, no missed patients

## Secondary Persona: Dr. Raj (Multi-Location Clinic Owner)

### Demographics
- **Age:** 40-55 years
- **Location:** Multiple cities (2-3 locations)
- **Practice Type:** Multi-location clinic with 5-10 doctors
- **Medical Experience:** 15+ years
- **Specialization:** Multiple specialties or chain of clinics
- **Monthly Revenue:** ₹5,00,000 - ₹20,00,000
- **Education:** MBBS, MD/MS, possibly MBA
- **Practice Setup:** Established business with staff

### Psychographics
- **Business Mindset:** Business-focused, growth-oriented, data-driven
- **Management Style:** Delegates operations, focuses on strategy
- **Innovation:** Early adopter of business tools, values efficiency
- **Scale Thinking:** Wants to standardize and scale operations
- **ROI Focused:** Makes decisions based on ROI and metrics

### Behavior Patterns

**Social Media Usage:**
- Has social media team or agency
- Multiple social accounts (one per location or specialty)
- High inquiry volume (100+ per week across all locations)
- Uses social media primarily for marketing
- Tracks metrics and performance
- Invests in social media advertising

**Practice Management:**
- Has receptionists at each location
- Uses practice management software
- Tracks KPIs (appointments, revenue, patient satisfaction)
- Standardizes processes across locations
- Focuses on operational efficiency

**Technology Usage:**
- Uses multiple business tools
- Comfortable with enterprise software
- Values integrations and automation
- Data-driven decision making
- Willing to invest in technology

### Pain Points

**1. Inconsistent Patient Experience (HIGH PRIORITY)**
- Different locations handle inquiries differently
- Inconsistent response times
- Varying quality of patient interaction
- Hard to maintain brand consistency
- **Impact:** Poor patient experience, brand damage

**2. High Operational Costs (HIGH PRIORITY)**
- Multiple receptionists = high salary costs
- Call center costs (if outsourced)
- Training overhead for new staff
- High staff turnover
- **Impact:** Reduced profitability, scaling challenges

**3. Lack of Centralized Tracking (MEDIUM PRIORITY)**
- Can't track inquiry sources across locations
- Don't know which marketing channels work
- Can't measure conversion rates
- No unified analytics
- **Impact:** Inefficient marketing spend, unclear ROI

**4. Scaling Challenges (MEDIUM PRIORITY)**
- Adding new locations = more staff
- Hard to maintain quality at scale
- Training new staff is time-consuming
- Standardization is difficult
- **Impact:** Slow growth, quality issues

**5. Staff Management (LOW PRIORITY)**
- Managing multiple receptionists
- Handling staff issues
- Training and onboarding
- Performance monitoring
- **Impact:** Time-consuming, operational overhead

### Goals & Motivations

**Primary Goals:**
1. **Standardize patient experience** - Consistent across all locations
2. **Reduce operational costs** - Automate where possible
3. **Scale efficiently** - Add locations without proportional cost increase
4. **Track performance** - Data-driven decisions
5. **Improve conversion** - Better inquiry to appointment conversion

**Motivations:**
- Business growth and profitability
- Operational efficiency
- Competitive advantage
- Data-driven insights
- Scalability

### Technology Comfort Level
- **Enterprise Software:** Very comfortable
- **Business Tools:** Very comfortable
- **Integrations:** Comfortable (values them)
- **Analytics:** Very comfortable (data-driven)
- **Technical Details:** Comfortable (can understand)

### Decision-Making Factors

**What Influences Purchase:**
1. **ROI** - Clear return on investment
2. **Scalability** - Works across multiple locations
3. **Analytics** - Provides insights and metrics
4. **Integration** - Works with existing systems
5. **Support** - Enterprise-level support

**Purchase Process:**
1. Identifies operational inefficiency
2. Evaluates multiple solutions
3. Considers ROI and total cost of ownership
4. Tests with pilot (one location)
5. Rolls out if successful

### Quote (Representative)
*"We have 3 locations and each one handles patient inquiries differently. Some are great, some are slow. I need a standardized solution that works the same way everywhere, gives me analytics on what's working, and reduces my operational costs. I'm willing to invest if it saves me money in the long run."*

### Use Case Scenario

**Current State:**
- 3 locations, 3 receptionists (₹30K each = ₹90K/month)
- Inconsistent response times
- Can't track which location gets most inquiries
- High staff turnover, constant training
- Operational overhead

**With Clariva Care:**
- 3 locations, 1 bot handles all (₹999/month)
- Consistent 24/7 responses
- Centralized dashboard with analytics
- No staff management overhead
- Clear ROI: Saves ₹89K/month
